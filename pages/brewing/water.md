# Water for Coffee Brewing — Malaga

---

## Why Water Matters

Coffee is ~98% water. The mineral content of water directly affects:
- **Extraction efficiency** — magnesium increases extraction; very soft water under-extracts
- **Taste** — high calcium = chalky/flat; high bicarbonate = muted acidity, flat cup
- **Equipment** — hard water scales kettles and machines

---

## Malaga Tap Water

Malaga tap water is sourced primarily from reservoirs (La Viñuela, Guadalhorce) and is treated with chlorine. It is:
- **Hard to very hard** — TDS typically 200–400 mg/L depending on season and zone
- **High in bicarbonates** — suppresses acidity, flattens specialty coffee
- **Chlorinated** — chlorine compounds negatively affect flavor

**Verdict:** Malaga tap water is not suitable for specialty coffee without treatment.

---

## Options

### 1. Filtered Water

**Approach:** Use a pitcher or inline filter that reduces hardness and chlorine.

- **Brita / BWT pitchers** — reduce chlorine, some hardness reduction. BWT filters add magnesium (beneficial for coffee). BWT is the better choice for coffee.
- **Reverse osmosis (RO)** — removes almost everything; requires remineralization if used for coffee (out of scope for this guide)

**Verdict:** A BWT pitcher is the simplest practical upgrade for home brewing in Malaga.

---

### 2. Bottled Water

Avoid highly mineralized "mineral water" (Lanjaron, Bezoya are popular but check TDS).

Ideal bottled water for coffee:
- **TDS:** 80–150 mg/L
- **Magnesium:** 10–30 mg/L
- **Bicarbonate:** below 70 mg/L (lower = better acidity clarity)
- **Chlorine:** none (bottled water doesn't have chlorine)

#### Candidates available in Spain

| Brand | TDS | Mg | HCO₃ | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Font Vella | ~170 | ~6 | ~72 | Borderline; low Mg |
| Volvic | ~109 | 8 | ~71 | Decent, widely available |
| Aquarel | low | low | low | Very soft — may underextract |
| Evian | ~357 | 26 | ~357 | Too high bicarbonate |

**Verdict:** Volvic is a reasonable off-the-shelf option if available. Font Vella is acceptable.

## SCA Water Standards (reference)

| Parameter | Target |
|---|---|
| TDS | 75–250 mg/L |
| Calcium hardness | 50–175 mg/L |
| Total alkalinity | ~40 mg/L as CaCO₃ |
| pH | 6.5–7.5 |
| No chlorine, no odor | — |
