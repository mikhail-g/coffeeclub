# Spotting Fake Specialty Coffee — Consumer Guide

---

## The Problem

"Specialty coffee" is not a protected term. Any roaster can print it on a bag. What matters is whether the coffee was actually scored 80+ by a Q Grader, sourced with traceability, and roasted to highlight (not hide) that quality.

This is a guide to reading a bag label critically.

---

## Green Flags (signals of genuine specialty)

- [ ] **Q Grader score listed** (e.g. "87.5 pts") — the gold standard
- [ ] **Farm or lot name** — not just country, but producer (e.g. "Finca La Palma, Huila, Colombia")
- [ ] **Harvest year** (e.g. "2024 harvest") — fresh crop matters
- [ ] **Roast date** (not best-before) — specialty roasters print when it was roasted
- [ ] **Processing method named** — Washed, Natural, Honey, Anaerobic
- [ ] **Variety named** — Bourbon, Gesha, Typica, SL28, etc.
- [ ] **Altitude listed** — e.g. "1,850 masl"
- [ ] **Flavor notes that make sense** — grounded in the origin/processing (not invented marketing)
- [ ] **Roast profile suggests light to medium** — specialty coffee isn't dark roasted
- [ ] **Transparent sourcing** — direct trade, importer named, or COE auction lot

---

## Red Flags (signals of marketing-grade specialty)

- [ ] **"Specialty" on the bag, nothing else to back it up**
- [ ] **Only country listed** — no region, farm, or producer (commodity-grade transparency)
- [ ] **No roast date** — only best-before (hides how old the coffee is)
- [ ] **Dark roast** — dark roasting destroys the qualities that make coffee specialty
- [ ] **Vague or implausible flavor notes** — "notes of sunshine and caramel" with no origin specifics
- [ ] **Blends without disclosed components** — a named blend is fine; a mystery blend is not
- [ ] **"Single origin" but no specifics** — origin without traceability is a half-truth
- [ ] **No processing method mentioned**
- [ ] **Price below ~€15–18/250g** — genuine specialty has real costs upstream; below this range, it's usually commercial grade

---

## Questions to Ask a Roaster

1. What score did this coffee receive? Who cupped it?
2. Can you tell me about the producer?
3. When was it roasted?
4. What's the harvest year?
5. How do you source — direct trade, importer, spot market?

A confident, specific answer is a green flag. Vagueness or defensiveness is a red flag.

---

## The "Specialty Wash" Pattern

Some roasters:
- Buy commercial-grade coffee
- Roast it dark (hides defects and low cup quality)
- Add evocative packaging and specialty vocabulary
- Charge specialty prices

The tell: dark roast + no score + no producer info + no roast date = commodity in specialty clothing.
