# The Full Chain: Origin to Cup

---

## Overview

Specialty coffee quality is built (or lost) at every stage of this chain:

```
Farm → Processing → Green Export → Roaster → Retailer → Brewer → Cup
```

Each stage has its own vocabulary, variables, and failure modes.

---

## 1. Origin

- **Country / Region / Farm / Lot** — specificity matters: a named farm or lot is more traceable and accountable than a country blend
- **Altitude** — higher elevation = slower cherry maturation = more complex sugars = more flavor potential
- **Variety** — Typica, Bourbon, Gesha, SL28, Pacamara, etc. Each has different flavor characteristics
- **Harvest** — hand-picked (selective) vs strip-picked; selective = only ripe cherries

## 2. Processing

How the coffee cherry is removed from the bean determines much of the cup's character.

| Method | Process | Flavor Profile |
|---|---|---|
| Washed (Natural Washed) | Pulp removed, fermented, dried on raised beds | Clean, bright, high acidity, terroir-forward |
| Natural (Dry) | Whole cherry dried in the sun | Fruity, heavy body, wine-like, fermented notes |
| Honey | Partial pulp left on during drying | Between washed and natural; sweetness, body |
| Anaerobic / Experimental | Fermented in sealed tanks | Distinctive, complex, sometimes polarizing |

---

## 3. Green Coffee Export & Grading

- Green coffee is graded by defect count, screen size, and cup quality
- SCA green grading: Specialty grade = max 5 full defects per 350g sample, zero category 1 defects
- Traceability: ideally traceable to farm/lot level, with harvest year

---

## 4. Roasting

- **Light roast** — preserves origin character, high acidity, complex
- **Medium roast** — balance of origin and roast character
- **Dark roast** — roast dominates, origin character diminished or lost
- Specialty coffee is typically roasted light to medium to let the coffee speak
- Roast date matters: coffee is best used **7–30 days post-roast** for filter
