# What Is Specialty Coffee?

---

## The SCA Definition

The Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) defines specialty coffee as coffee that scores **80 points or above** on a 100-point scale when evaluated by a certified Q Grader using the SCA cupping protocol.

Coffee scoring:
- **80–84** — Specialty (Very Good)
- **85–89** — Excellent
- **90+** — Outstanding / rare

Below 80 points is commercial-grade coffee, regardless of what the bag says.

---

## The SCA Cupping Protocol

Cupping is a standardized tasting method used to evaluate coffee objectively.

### What is scored (10 categories, each 0–10 points):
1. **Fragrance / Aroma** — dry grounds + wet bloom
2. **Flavor** — overall taste impression
3. **Aftertaste** — length and quality of finish
4. **Acidity** — brightness, liveliness (not sourness)
5. **Body** — mouthfeel, weight
6. **Balance** — how the attributes work together
7. **Uniformity** — consistency across 5 cups
8. **Clean Cup** — absence of defects
9. **Sweetness** — natural sweetness, absence of harshness
10. **Overall** — holistic impression

### Defects (subtract from score):
- **Taint** (Category 1 defect): -2 points per occurrence
- **Fault** (Category 2 defect): -4 points per occurrence

---

## What This Means in Practice

A coffee scoring 80+ has been:
- Grown at altitude with care for variety and processing
- Evaluated green (unroasted) for physical defects
- Roasted and cupped by trained evaluators
- Found to have **no significant defects** and **distinguishable positive attributes**
