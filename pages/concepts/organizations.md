# Organizations: SCA & ECBC

---

## SCA — Specialty Coffee Association

**Website:** sca.coffee

The SCA is the global trade body for specialty coffee. It sets industry standards, runs education programs, and organizes the World Coffee Championships.

### What the SCA does:
- Defines the **80-point threshold** for specialty coffee
- Maintains the **Q Grader** certification (professional coffee evaluators)
- Publishes brewing standards (water, ratio, extraction yield, TDS)
- Runs the **World Barista Championship**, **Brewers Cup**, **Cup Tasters Championship**
- Offers a structured **Coffee Skills Program** (CSP) — courses from Barista to Roasting to Sensory

### Why it matters to you:
- When a roaster claims specialty, the SCA framework is what that claim should be measured against
- The Q Grader score on a bag is the most credible signal of cup quality
- SCA brewing standards give you a calibrated baseline for your own recipes

### SCA Brewing Standards (filter coffee):
- **Ratio:** 55g/L (±10%)
- **Water temperature:** 90–96°C
- **Extraction yield:** 18–22%
- **TDS (strength):** 1.15–1.45%

## ECBC — European Coffee Brewing Centre

**Website:** ecbc.eu (based in Oslo, Norway — founded by the NCA, Norwegian Coffee Association)

The ECBC focuses specifically on **brewed coffee quality** and **brewing equipment certification**.

### What the ECBC does:
- Certifies home and professional brewing equipment (the **ECBC Approved** label)
- Publishes a European brewing standard aligned with SCA parameters
- Runs research into water, extraction, and equipment performance

### Why it matters to you:
- If you're evaluating a coffee maker or kettle, ECBC certification is a credible signal
- Their brewing parameters closely mirror SCA — useful cross-reference
- Less directly relevant for filter/manual brewing (AeroPress, Clever), more for automated machines

## Other Relevant Bodies

| Organization | Focus |
|---|---|
| **COE (Cup of Excellence)** | Annual competition identifying top lots per country; lots sold at auction with full traceability |
| **BOP (Best of Panama)** | Panama's equivalent; famous for Gesha discoveries |
| **Rainforest Alliance / UTZ** | Sustainability certifications — not a quality score, but an ethics signal |
| **Fair Trade** | Economic fairness for farmers — again, not quality, but worth understanding |
